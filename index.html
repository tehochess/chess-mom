Golden Rules of Chess

♟

Control the center

Put your pawns and pieces where they can control the four central squares. Whoever owns the center controls the game.

🚀

Develop your pieces early

Get your knights and bishops off the back rank in the first few moves. Pieces stuck at home can't help you.

🏰

Castle early to protect your king

Castling tucks your king safely behind pawns and connects your rooks. Try to castle within the first 10 moves.

🚫

Don't move the same piece twice in the opening

Every move in the opening should bring out a new piece. Moving the same piece twice wastes a turn your opponent can use.

🔗

Connect your rooks

After castling and developing, clear the back rank so your rooks guard each other. Rooks double in strength when working together.

🤔

Think before you move

Before every move ask: is my piece safe where I'm moving it? Am I leaving anything undefended? Can my opponent capture something for free?

⚖️

Trade pieces wisely

Don't give away a bishop or knight for a pawn. Know the values: pawn = 1, knight/bishop = 3, rook = 5, queen = 9.

👑

Activate your king in the endgame

Once most pieces are off the board, the king becomes a strong piece. March it to the center to help your pawns promote.
